  1. 21 CFR Part 11  — Electronic Records and Signatures
  2. 21 CFR Part 50  — Human Subject Protection / Informed Consent
  3. 21 CFR Part 56  — Institutional Review Boards (IRBs)
  4. 21 CFR Part 58  — Good Laboratory Practice (GLP)
  5. 21 CFR Part 211 — Current Good Manufacturing Practice (cGMP)
  6. 21 CFR Part 312 — Investigational New Drug (IND) Applications
  7. 21 CFR Part 314 — NDA/ANDA Applications
  8. 45 CFR Part 46  — Common Rule (HHS Human Subject Protections)

SAMPLE PROGRESSION:
  Sample 1 (0/8): Bare-bones draft. Scientific content only. Zero regulatory
                  language. CLARA should flag all 8 regulations.

                  and IND sponsor mention (Part 312). Still missing 6 regulations.
                  CLARA should flag missing elements within the 2 addressed
                  regulations plus all 6 unaddressed ones.

  Sample 3 (5/8): After second feedback. Adds IRB (Part 56), AE reporting
                  (Part 312 expanded), GLP (Part 58), cGMP (Part 211). Improves
                  consent and IND sections. Still missing Part 11, Part 314,

  Sample 4 (7/8): Near-final. Adds electronic records (Part 11) and Common Rule
                  (45 CFR 46). Substantially improves all prior sections. Only
                  Part 314 (NDA pathway) remains minimal — which is acceptable
                  for Phase 1 but could be flagged.

  Sample 5 (8/8): Fully compliant. All regulations addressed with appropriate
                  depth. Mirrors the real protocol's regulatory completeness.
                  CLARA should return minimal or no findings.

EXPECTED CLARA BEHAVIOR:
  Each sample should produce progressively fewer findings as compliance improves.
  The specific gaps are annotated at the bottom of each sample file for manual
  verification of CLARA's detection accuracy.

USAGE:
  Feed each sample into the CLARA pipeline and compare:
  1. Number of findings per sample (should decrease: S1 >> S2 > S3 > S4 > S5)
  2. Specificity of findings (CLARA should identify the exact missing elements)
  3. False positives on Sample 5 (should be minimal)
  4. False negatives across all samples (gaps CLARA misses)
